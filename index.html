I am a PhD student in the Cognition & Perception program at NYU. I work primarily with Molly Dillon and Marjorie Rhodes. I started my PhD in 2022. Before that, I was at Temple University, where I studied Philosophy and Psychology, and worked as a research assistant with Tim Shipley in the Research in Spatial Cognition (RISC) lab.

I am broadly interested in the relationship between language and conceptual development.


Projects

One of my projects with Molly Dillon looks at how language relates to conceptions of agency and experience, and how these are related to information about gaze direction. Another project looks at what aspects of others' actions adults and children use to make guesses about the meanings of novel words. For example, do people learning novel verbs make use of the efficiency, object-directedness, and path trajectories of others' actions? And does this change if, given the same information, people are learning novel nouns instead?

In other work with Marjorie Rhodes and Eric Mandelbaum, I'm looking at memory asymmetries in whether children remember denied statements. We are interested in whether young children misremember denied statements as confirmed ones early in development. Previous work has found that, when placed under cognitive load, adults misremember denied statements as true more often than they misremembered confirmed statements as false. We are interested in the development of this phenomena as a window into the mechanisms that explain it.
